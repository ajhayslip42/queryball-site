You've heard it on every Sunday broadcast: *"They're 8 for 14 on third down today, that's huge."* It is huge — for the highlight package. As a predictor of anything, it's noise.

Here's why, and what to slice for instead.

## The number conflates two completely different problems

A third-down conversion is the joint outcome of two independent challenges:

1. **Did you put yourself in a manageable third down?** A team that's 6 for 8 on 3rd-and-3 is not the same team as one that's 6 for 8 on 3rd-and-9. Both go on the broadcast as "75%." Neither situation will recur with the same distribution next week.
2. **Did you execute the third-down play?** Given the distance, were you above or below expectation?

Mash those together and you get a number that bounces between 30% and 60% on a 14-play sample, week to week, with most of the variance driven by which buckets you happened to land in.

## What to look at instead

Two splits do more work than the bulk number ever will:

- **Distance distribution on third down.** This is a 1st- and 2nd-down quality metric in disguise. Teams that consistently face 3rd-and-3 are good on the early downs. Teams that face 3rd-and-9 aren't, no matter what their conversion rate looks like.
- **EPA per play on third down, conditional on distance.** This isolates execution from situation. A team at +0.15 EPA/play on 3rd-and-medium is doing real work. A team at +0.04 with a flattering conversion% is getting lucky on short.

The decks let you pull both of these in one click. Set Down = 3, then look at distance distribution, EPA, success rate, and conversion rate side by side. Two different teams with the same 42% conversion can look entirely different here — and the one that looks better is the one that's going to keep doing it.

The conversion percentage isn't going anywhere on the broadcast. But you don't have to use it for anything important.
